AI Art System - Quick Start Guide

Definitions:

  • Vibe: The overall mood or emotional tone that the song clip conveys. What is the overall mood you feel from the song clip?
  • Imagery: The visual elements or concepts inspired by the song, such as patterns, objects, or scenes.
  • Style: The artistic approach or design aesthetic used in the visuals, like abstract, realistic, or retro.
  • Color: The primary color palette that defines the visuals, influenced by the song's mood or energy. Note: this is subject to change and hard to control in AI models.
    • Recommended: black and white -> sudden introduction of color at beat drop or crescendo
  • Motion: The type of camera movement applied to the video, such as 2D (panning) or 3D (rotational)
    • Default: 3D
  • Strength: Defines the strength of the motion, how much you feel the visual change. Varies from weak, normal, strong to vstrong
  • Note: Except for Strength and Motion, all other inputs can have values other than those in the dropdown menus. These are just suggestions.
  • Intervals: These are the main scene changes in your music video. You assign some vibe, color palette, texture, imagery and style and the AI video generator will use these to generate a continuous video that moves through the music.
    • Defaults are attached at relatively evenly spaced intervals and try to attach either to a nearby beat or low-point in the song where it makes sense to change scenes.
    • Note: lines were drawn thick for visibility, but the left-most point on the green intervals is where the scene change will take place
    • Recommended: Keep texture and style consistent throughout scene changes, imagery and color are subject to change.
  • Transitions: Dramatic visual or motion shifts added to sync with the music and make it more audio-reactive or introduce visual change.
  • Initial Image: The starting visual reference that sets the tone or theme for the generated video. Note: AI generator will not adhere closely to the image, but will use the color palette and texture as suggestions.

Step 1: Upload Your Audio Clip

  • Click on the “Upload Audio” button.
  • Select an audio file from your device (supported formats: .mp3, .wav, .m4a).

Step 2: Add Interval Segments

  • You will be prompted to add or delete intervals. Move transitions to ideal locations when new imagery, or feeling is to be expressed and align left side of interval to point of visual change.
  • Techniques:
    • 1) Align intervals to beats or low points in the song and change imagery or color (especially good if the vibe of the song suddenly changes)
    • 2) Align intervals at even locations for less dynamic songs and use transitions to incorporate visual change

Step 3: Add Transition Segments

  • You will be prompted to manually add transition motions or fill with defaults. Default transitions give 1 second around each interval to indicate a scene change occuring and 1.5 seconds at the end for big and impactful ending motion (Recommended: vstrong rotation/spin or zoom)
  • Tips:
    • If transition time is short, ensure strong or vstrong strength to ensure they can be felt, or give longer time for the transitions.
    • Recommendations:
      • Upward pan or rotate on crescendos or upon increase in song's energy
      • Use zoom_in with sinusoidal strength for swerving effect. Reference Here.
  • Press Finalize Timestamps button when done

Step 4: Feel The Music

  • Feel the Music and determine an accurate overall vibe, imagery, texture and color scheme. This will auto-populate the final table which breaks down each interval and transition's input parameters.

Step 5: Choose an Initial Image

  • To the right of the "Input Details" box is an image gallery with some popular image and texture combinations to use as initial images. Click on one to populate the texture and imagery input boxes.
  • If you prefer to create your own, use the Brainstorming section to generate images from textual prompt or upload an image directly. Focus on color palettes, textures, and vibes when selecting an initial image.
  • Press Fill With Defaults

Step 6: Finalize Prompt Parameter Table

  • Modify inputs in the table as needed to match the overall scene you want to be represented for each chunk of the song.
  • Preset Defaults (subject to change for artistic vision):
    • Carry over vibe, imagery, texture, style and color for all boxes
    • Motion for scene intervals is zoom in by default
    • Motion for transitions is strong rotate right (3D) or pan right (2D) by default
    • Final transition is a very strong rotation (3D) or spin (2D)
    • Motion and Strength can have multiple different motions at the same time
      • Motion: zoom_in,pan_right
      • Stength: vstrong,10*sin(2*3.14*t/10)
  • Press Create Video. Final output will have the inputs passed into the AI model along with a link to the generated video and a link with the video automatically stitched with the input audio.
Additional Tips:
Experiment with different transition effects and segment lengths to enhance creativity.
